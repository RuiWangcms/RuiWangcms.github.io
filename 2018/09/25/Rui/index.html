Calculate carriers' effective mass from the band structure diagram

Posted on 2018-09-25

This post describes how to get the carriers’ effective mass in a computational way. To achieve this, the band structure should have been calculated through CASTEP or VASP.

Overview of steps

  1. Calculate the band structure diagram and get the result file.
  2. Find the particular part band that you care about and employ units transformation.
  3. Fit the band with a quadratic function, $y = a + bx + cx^2$.
  4. The effective mass of carriers could be written as $m = \frac{1}{2c}m_0$.

Step1: Plot the band structure

This step is common. Calculate the band structure and plot it. Here is an example of SiC in X direction.
In the following parts, I will show how to get the effective mass of electrons.

Step2: Units transformation

In the current diagram, the unit of Y axis is $eV$ while the unit of X axis is $\overset{\circ}{A}$. In order to simplify the fitting process, the units transformation is needed. $eV$ is transformed into $Hartree$ and the unit of X, meaning the path in K space, is converted to $Born^{-1}$.
The relationships are $1 Hartree = 27.21 eV, 1 Born = 0.5292 \overset{\circ}{A}$

Step3: Quadratic function fitting

After the units transformation, we can fit the specific band with a quadratic functin like $y = a + bx + cx^2$. It should be stated that the fitting coefficients depend on many factors, the mesh points in K space, the number of fitting points and so on. If you want to use this method to get the effective mass, you should choose higher calculation parameters.

Step4: Get the effective mass

The definition of effective mass is we can get the result, $m_{n}^{*} = \frac{1}{2c}m_{0}$, and c is the coefficient of $x^{2}$. The Planck constant has been included in the second derivative through the units transforamtion.
